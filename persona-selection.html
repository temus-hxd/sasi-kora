Introducing Healthier SG Screening

Introduce to 70 year-old Uncle Lim the benefits of Healthier SG Screening and how he can access affordable screenings at nearby clinics.

Scenario Summary

Introduce to 70 year-old Uncle Lim the benefits of Healthier SG Screening and how he can access affordable screenings at nearby clinics.

Persona Bio: Lim Swee Heng (Age 70)

  • Lives alone in a 3-room HDB flat
  • Has high blood pressure and mild knee pain
  • Not currently enrolled in Healthier SG
  • Retired hawker, managing on savings and some CPF payouts
  • Son works overseas, visits once yearly during Chinese New Year
  • Friendly but somewhat shy about discussing health matters

Training Goals

  • Practice conducting a health and social needs assessment
  • Introduce Healthier SG screening benefits and registration process
  • Practice supportive communication techniques

Suggested Dialogue Flow

1

Stage 1: Warm Introduction and Building Trust

Start with friendly small talk about daily routine and family to help Uncle Lim feel comfortable before discussing health matters.

Technique: Use clear announcements: "Uncle Lim, I am here today to chat with you about your health and see how we can support you. Is that okay with you?"

2

Stage 2: Health Assessment

Gently ask about current health conditions, medications, and last health screening, noting the high blood pressure and knee pain concerns.

Technique: Use yes/no questions and nouns instead of pronouns: "Uncle Lim, do you have high blood pressure? Does Uncle Lim take medicine for the high blood pressure every day?"

3

Stage 3: Introduce Healthier SG Screening Benefits

Explain how enrolling in Healthier SG can provide subsidised screenings (as low as $25 for Pioneer Generation) at a regular clinic near his home.

Technique: Use right-branching sentences: "Healthier SG helps Uncle Lim with health screening. Health screening checks Uncle Lim's blood pressure, checks Uncle Lim's sugar level, checks Uncle Lim's cholesterol. The screening costs only $25 for Uncle Lim."

4

Stage 4: Social and Financial Needs Check

Ask about social activities, loneliness, financial concerns, and whether he knows about home modification programmes or community activities nearby.

Technique: Use matching comments: "Yes, meeting friends at the market is good for Uncle Lim."

5

Stage 5: Next Steps and Follow-up

Summarise what was discussed, provide contact information for Healthier SG enrollment, and arrange a follow-up visit or connect him to relevant services.

Technique: Use positive instructions: "Uncle Lim can call this number to sign up for Healthier SG. Good!"

Person-Centered Care Communication Techniques

Building Connection and Trust

  • Active Listening - Give full attention and show understanding
  • Asking Permission - "Is it okay if we talk about health screening?"
  • Politeness - Use respectful language and titles
  • Announcing Activity/Intent - State clearly what will happen next

Asking Questions Effectively

  • Open-Ended Questions - "What does Uncle Lim do in the morning?"
  • Yes/No Questions - "Does Uncle Lim have knee pain?"

Speaking Clearly and Simply

  • Address by Name - Say "Uncle Lim" instead of "you"
  • Right-Branching Sentences - Put main idea first, details after
  • Positive Instructions - Say what to do, not what not to do

Showing Understanding and Support

  • Allowing Time to Respond - Wait patiently for 5-10 seconds
  • Matching Comments - Repeat back what was said to show understanding
  • Affirmations - "Yes, that is right" or "Good, Uncle Lim understands"

Befriending Visit

Practice meaningful small talk and companionship with 70-year-old Lim Swee Heng during a befriending visit to help him have enjoyable company.

Scenario Summary

Practice meaningful small talk and companionship with 70-year-old Lim Swee Heng during a befriending visit to help him have enjoyable company.

Persona Bio: Lim Swee Heng (Age 70)

  • Lives alone in a 3-room HDB flat
  • Has high blood pressure and mild knee pain
  • Not currently enrolled in Healthier SG
  • Retired hawker, managing on savings and some CPF payouts
  • Son works overseas, visits once yearly during Chinese New Year
  • Friendly but somewhat shy about discussing health matters

Training Goals

  • Practice building genuine connection through relaxed conversation that makes Lim Swee Heng feel valued and heard
  • Practice small talk topics that bring joy and meaning to the senior, such as memories, hobbies, family, and daily routines

Suggested Dialogue Flow

1

Stage 1: Warm Greeting and Settling In

Begin with a friendly greeting and some light conversation about the weather or the neighbourhood to help Mr. Lim feel comfortable with the visit.

Technique: Use announcing activity and politeness: "Mr. Lim, I am here today to spend time with Mr. Lim and have a nice chat. May I sit here with Mr. Lim?"

2

Stage 2: Exploring Daily Life and Interests

Ask about Mr. Lim's daily routine, hobbies, and what he enjoys doing, showing genuine interest in his answers and encouraging him to share more.

Technique: Use open-ended questions: "What does Mr. Lim like to do in the morning? What food does Mr. Lim enjoy cooking?"

3

Stage 3: Sharing Stories and Memories

Encourage Mr. Lim to share stories about his hawker days, family memories, or life in the neighbourhood, listening attentively and responding with interest.

Technique: Use matching comments and allowing time to respond: "Yes, working as a hawker must have been very busy. Mr. Lim worked hard for many years." Then wait patiently for Mr. Lim to continue.

4

Stage 4: Finding Common Ground and Light Topics

Discuss shared interests such as favourite foods, local events, television programmes, or news from the neighbourhood to create connection and enjoyment.

Technique: Use affirmations: "Yes, that kopitiam makes very good kopi. Mr. Lim has good taste!"

5

Stage 5: Gentle Closing and Planning Next Visit

Thank Mr. Lim for the pleasant conversation, summarise what was enjoyed during the visit, and arrange when to visit again.

Technique: Use positive instructions: "Mr. Lim, thank you for sharing stories with me today. I enjoyed hearing about Mr. Lim's hawker stall. I will come visit Mr. Lim again next week. Is Tuesday morning good for Mr. Lim?"

Person-Centered Care Communication Techniques

Building Connection and Trust

  • Active Listening - Give full attention and show understanding
  • Asking Permission - "May I sit here with Mr. Lim?"
  • Politeness - Use respectful language and titles
  • Announcing Activity/Intent - State clearly what will happen next

Asking Questions Effectively

  • Open-Ended Questions - "What does Mr. Lim enjoy doing?" or "What was Mr. Lim's favourite dish to cook?"
  • Yes/No Questions - "Does Mr. Lim watch the evening news?"

Speaking Clearly and Simply

  • Using Nouns Instead of Pronouns - Say "Mr. Lim" instead of "you"
  • Right-Branching Sentences - Put main idea first, details after
  • Positive Instructions - Say what to do, not what not to do

Showing Understanding and Support

  • Allowing Time to Respond - Wait patiently for 5-10 seconds, especially during storytelling
  • Matching Comments - Repeat back what was said to show understanding
  • Affirmations - "Yes, that is interesting" or "Mr. Lim tells good stories"

Introducing Advance Care Planning

Introduce to 70-year-old Lim Swee Heng the benefits of Advance Care Planning and guide him on how to book an ACP appointment with a certified provider.

Scenario Summary

Introduce to 70-year-old Lim Swee Heng the benefits of Advance Care Planning and guide him on how to book an ACP appointment with a certified provider.

Persona Bio: Lim Swee Heng (Age 70)

  • Lives alone in a 3-room HDB flat
  • Has high blood pressure and mild knee pain
  • Not currently enrolled in Healthier SG
  • Retired hawker, managing on savings and some CPF payouts
  • Son works overseas, visits once yearly during Chinese New Year
  • Friendly but somewhat shy about discussing health matters

Training Goals

  • Practice introducing Advance Care Planning in a sensitive manner
  • Explain ACP benefits clearly and guide the senior on how to book an ACP appointment with a certified provider

Suggested Dialogue Flow

1

Stage 1: Warm Introduction and Building Trust

Start with friendly small talk about daily routine and health management before gently introducing the topic of planning for future healthcare.

Technique: Use clear announcements and asking permission: "Mr. Lim, I would like to talk with Mr. Lim about planning for future health care. Is that okay with Mr. Lim?"

2

Stage 2: Introduce What ACP Is

Explain that Advance Care Planning helps Mr. Lim tell doctors and family what kind of healthcare Mr. Lim wants if Mr. Lim becomes very sick and cannot speak for himself.

Technique: Use right-branching sentences: "Advance Care Planning helps Mr. Lim make choices. Mr. Lim can choose what treatment Mr. Lim wants. Mr. Lim can tell family members what is important to Mr. Lim."

3

Stage 3: Explain ACP Benefits

Share how ACP gives Mr. Lim control, reduces worry for family members, and ensures doctors understand Mr. Lim's wishes about comfort care, life support, and where Mr. Lim wants to receive care.

Technique: Use yes/no questions: "Does Mr. Lim want family to know Mr. Lim's healthcare wishes? Does Mr. Lim want to choose someone to speak for Mr. Lim if Mr. Lim cannot speak?"

4

Stage 4: Address Concerns and Answer Questions

Listen to any worries Mr. Lim has about discussing end-of-life planning, and reassure him that ACP can be changed anytime and is about living well, not about dying.

Technique: Use matching comments and affirmations: "Yes, Mr. Lim is right. ACP can be changed anytime. Good that Mr. Lim asks this question."

5

Stage 5: Guide on Booking ACP Appointment

Explain where Mr. Lim can go for ACP (polyclinics, hospitals, community centres), provide contact information, and offer to help Mr. Lim make the appointment or arrange a callback.

Technique: Use positive instructions: "Mr. Lim can call this number to book ACP appointment. Mr. Lim can also visit the community centre near Mr. Lim's home. Would Mr. Lim like me to help Mr. Lim call now?"

Person-Centered Care Communication Techniques

Building Connection and Trust

  • Active Listening - Give full attention and show understanding
  • Asking Permission - "Is it okay if we talk about Advance Care Planning?"
  • Politeness - Use respectful language and titles
  • Announcing Activity/Intent - State clearly what will happen next

Asking Questions Effectively

  • Open-Ended Questions - "What is important to Mr. Lim when thinking about healthcare?"
  • Yes/No Questions - "Does Mr. Lim have someone Mr. Lim trusts to make decisions?"

Speaking Clearly and Simply

  • Address by name - Say "Mr. Lim" instead of "you"
  • Right-Branching Sentences - Put main idea first, details after
  • Positive Instructions - Say what to do, not what not to do

Showing Understanding and Support

  • Allowing Time to Respond - Wait patiently for 5-10 seconds
  • Matching Comments - Repeat back what was said to show understanding
  • Affirmations - "Yes, that is right" or "Good, Mr. Lim understands"
